Miles Davis

The Prince of Darkness

Miles' Story:

  • 1926 - Born in Alton, Illinois.
  • 1936 - Begins classical violin lessons.
  • 1939 - Receives his first trumpet, on his 13th Birthday and begins lessons with local trumpeter Elwood Buchanan.
  • 1942 - On his 13th Birthday, receives his first trumpet and begins lessons with local trumpeter Elwood Buchanan.
  • 1943 - Joins Eddie Randle's Blue Devils, a local big band in St. Louis, his first sideman role.
  • 1944 - Subbed for the Billy Eckstine Big Band, exposing Davis to bebop. The ensemble includes Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, and Sarah Vaughan. In September, arrives in New York City to attend the Juilliard School of Music but also to reconnect with Parker and Gillespie.
  • 1945 - Splits his time between attending music school and jamming in various clubs downtown. He later drops out of Juilliard to focus on modern Jazz.
  • 1945 - Splits his time between attending music school and jamming in various clubs downtown. He later drops out of Juilliard to focus on modern Jazz.
  • 1947 - Records as leader for the first time for Savoy Records.
  • 1948 - Continues to perform, travel and record as a member of Parker's groundbreaking quintent. The nonet project, later to be called Birth of the Cool, is generated by a group of musicians Davis spends time with in Gil Evans' basement apartment. He turns down an offer of a chair in the Duke Ellington Big Band, and quits Parker's band at the end of the year.
  • 1949 - Spends most of the year leading his nonet, and releases Birth of the Cool, his first truly influential project as a leader. In June, he falls into a depression and turns to heroin.
  • 1950 - The beginning of Davis' self-described "four-year horror show".
  • 1954 - Returns to New York City, after kicking his drug dependency.
  • 1955 - Miles' solo is the talk of the Newport Jazz Festival, and the head of album production at Columbia Records finally agrees to Miles recording for the major label. Forms the first great quintent of Coltrane, Garland, Chambers and Jones.
  • 1957 - Officially becomes a Columbia Records recoring artist, a relationship that will last 30 years.
  • 1960 - Miles tours with Coltrane under the Jazz at the Phiharmonic banner, marking the first time Miles tours overseas with his own band. For the rest of his career, he will never perform on tour with any band but his own, except a few special tributes.
  • 1961 - Wins his first career GRAMMY, in the category of Best Jazz Composition of More THan Five Minutes Duration, for Sketches of Spain.
  • 1962 - Inducted into the DownBeat Jazz Hall of Fame.
  • 1964 - Performs a concert at Lincoln Center's Philharmonic Hall as a benefit for various organisations involved with the ongoing Civil Rights effort and voter registration drives.
  • 1965 - Enters the hospital due to an ongoing struggle with sickle cell anemia and premature arthritis, which becomes a general affliction Miles struggles with for the rest of his career.
  • 1971 - Receives his second GRAMMY for Best Jazz Performance - Large Group, or Soloist with Large Group, for Bitches Brew.
  • 1975 - Performs his last concert before a forced haitus due to a long-needed hip operation.
  • 1981 - Performs publicy for the first time since 1975 with his new band in the Boston nightclub Kix, an open rehearsal for his grand debut at the Kool Jazz Festival in early July.
  • 1982 - "Birth of The Cool" is inducted into the GRAMMY Hall of Fame.
  • 1985 - Signs with Warner Bros. Records, ending a 30-year relationship with Columbia.
  • 1986 - Becomes a Honorary Doctor of Music at the New England Conservatory.
  • 1990 - Receives a Lifetime Achievement GRAMMY.
  • 1991 - Embarks on his last tour overseas in France, the first country he experienced outside of the US. He later performs at the Hollywood Bowl, his last concert. He passes away on September 28th.
  • 2006 - Inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame.
